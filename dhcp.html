What is a lease?

Temporary ownership

DHCP addresses are rented, not owned. Your router assigns IPs for a set duration — typically 24 hours. When 50% of the lease expires, your device quietly renegotiates before it runs out.

Why UDP?

Bootstrap problem

TCP requires an IP address to open a connection — but the whole point of DHCP is to get one. UDP broadcasts bypass this chicken-and-egg problem. Ports 67 (server) and 68 (client) are reserved.

Multiple servers

The Request broadcast

Step 3 is a broadcast even though the client already knows its server. This is intentional: other DHCP servers on the network see the request and withdraw their competing offers, freeing up those IPs.
